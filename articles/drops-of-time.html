Drops Of Time

She was amidst the white and grey tombstones in the middle of the graveyard. There was no mourning no funeral but an urge to feel and touch peace. It was a need to sit in calm surroundings rummage through the book of life  and what better place than that where no one talked, no one judged, no one cheated .There were no threats, no desires. The horizontally placed bodies in neat enclosures gave a silent support as though saying ‘we know what you are going through, for we have been through all this ‘. She felt reassured in that vast quietitude where a segment lay lifeless, resting in wisdom and experience. She usually went there to get affirmations when in doubt.

And she did get answers.

Her apartment oversaw the cemetery and very often she looked out of her window to communicate with that undefined, unknown community. The eerie silence would be comforting.

She was not alone.

The souls danced in liberty and peace. . .and so did she.

Travelling back in time can be therapeutic. It is like a purification process in the fire of self-assessment. She did it often for this was an exercise in acceptance, important in all therapies.

How pretty her daughter was , but how naïve !She lacked worldly wisdom and Aarti after her divorce had become too protective towards her daughter .She wanted to caution her without scaring , advise her without moralizing and at times used to wonder if she is passing her insecurities and fears to her . So just chose to listen to her and soak it all in.

 So great was her awe for Sara that Maya  happily welcomed the 'other women' in her life with an élan and an ease which was unfathomable. It foxed her friends. How could she, when most of the women would have clawed their husbands with unforgiving gestures, caustic comments and rabid behaviour. In a marital domain slightest flings can lead to suspicions.

Maya stayed unaware, for Sara navigated this slippery terrain of adultery, good time, money and sex with great intelligence and secrecy. Her husband Jehangir Khan was a well-connected politician, and everyone wanted to play safe. Sara loved her status and enjoyed arranging drop-of-a-hat parties, films and other activities that promised great fun. Probably she lacked security and a feeling of wantedness and loved to be fussed over. Strangely enough synthetic alliances fall pale when pitted against ones that have a purity of purpose, for the latter have an energy that draws people instantly towards oneself. Surely he can call me. At least to check up on my face. She tried his phone again and this time it rang .

Hello!! Hello!!Who is this please and the phone got disconnected after a brief hello. She willed herself to not check her phone to see if he had replied. It had been about three days now. She hated that she was constantly checking his 'last seen at' status and yes, he had logged in just five minutes ago. Yet she couldn't stop herself. This sinking feeling to find absolutely no communication from him was becoming unbearable, almost torturous.

And then, just as she sat down in her chair, her phone vibrated. With her heart thudding in her ear, she unlocked her phone and stared at the screen. Finally! It was his message.

But when she opened it and read it, she nearly stopped breathing. She didn't know if he was joking or not.

He was with Sara?

She finished the bath and came out with a pounding headache. The water was truly hot. Rolled in a towel she stepped out carefully.

She sat down after brushing her wet hair and touched the scar on her face. It had healed and many told her that it added to her charm, especially when she smiled.

Wisdom lines, wisdom scars.

Going back in time helped one in handling one’s anguish and pain. Like any other revisions in schools, it only made one better and stronger at a subject.

She remembered a passage from a book that she was reading.

 

‘There is a sense of calm and peace as karma postures itself. Karma is not only dishing out good for good and evil for evil; it also stands for accepting the choices one makes in life. Karma means attaining a poise in life , when one sits back and reflects on ones perceptions and attitudes, accepts them  and owns up all that follows.’

Arun had called her up on several occasions expressing his desire to get back as Sara was not only overbearing but had involved him in a fraud. The channels were talking about how the two were involved in a  major ‘embezzlement’ and a big case was being made against them. Jehangir Khan had disowned his wife as he didn’t want the scandal to scathe his reputation .She kept seeing Aruns face on the TV from time to time and it surprised her that she was watching it with a calm curiosity .Reporters would call up frequently for an interview from the ex-wife which she would decline even before they’d ask her.

If love is blinding, betrayal is strengthening. It is ironical that pain and bitterness furnish one’s inner core with a resilience that is hard to beat.

There comes in a calm, a resolve that is defining and definite. Probably love is an overrated emotion a debilitating phenomenon, a superfluous emotion glorified over the years by poets and writers, a hormonal flux dotted with lust,  greed and selfishness .It is expensive too ,as it costs peace and dignity. It was his turn now. It was his chosen course; a price to pay for love, his karma. Life had plans for him.

…………………………………………………………

The shadows lengthened, the place turned darker, the somber sky with the fading lights gave the cemetery a cold, forsaken look. An occasional stir in the nearby bushes alerted her .She waited .She had conquered love and defeated her failings. She had reinvented herself. She was in line with life Maya heard it again.  Turning around she clearly saw her mother’s shadow. this time she looked more complete and resolute as though congratulating her for her renewed energy, a confidence that Maya was empowered to live her life with meaning and substance. She was the deity with many arms, much power. She could feel the drops of time, enriching the graves. She smiled at the shadow, was tempted to hug it but instead turned back and hurried her pace.
